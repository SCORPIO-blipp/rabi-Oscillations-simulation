PART I Background Reading

Read carefully before opening the simulation.

The Classical Analogy : A Playground Swing

Imagine pushing a child on a swing. If you push at random times, very little energy transfers. But if you push at exactly the right moment every cycle, the swing gets higher and higher. You are driving the system at its natural (resonant) frequency. This is resonance: maximum energy transfer when the driving frequency matches the system's natural frequency.

The Quantum Twist : A Qubit

A qubit is a quantum two-level system. Instead of 0 or 1, it can be in a superposition of both states simultaneously. When a laser or microwave pulse is tuned to the qubit's transition frequency, the qubit does not just gain energy like a swing. It cycles its state smoothly between |0⟩ and |1⟩. The rate of this cycling is the Rabi frequency (Ω). Turning the laser off mid-cycle freezes the qubit in superposition. This is a Rabi oscillation.
